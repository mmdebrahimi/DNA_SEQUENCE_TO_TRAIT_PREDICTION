## Honest gaps

- **EnteroBase per-record label-provenance field** — no documentation found indicating whether EnteroBase stores a field like `pathotype_source = BlastFrost | manual | publication`. The 2020 user's guide and 2022 HierCC paper both reference BlastFrost but don't document a label-provenance metadata field. Direct contact with EnteroBase maintainers (Mark Achtman group, Warwick) would be required to determine this definitively.
- **NCBI Pathogen Detection pathotype field** — confirmed absent in the help docs; whether `host_disease` or similar BioSample attributes are populated densely enough for E. coli to serve as independent-label proxies requires querying the NCBI Pathogen Detection isolate-browser SOLR index directly (e.g., facet counts on `host_disease` for E. coli isolates).
- **DECA collection per-pathotype breakdown** — abstract states "15 most common diarrheagenic clones" but per-clone strain counts and per-pathotype totals (EPEC vs EHEC vs ETEC vs EAEC vs EIEC) are not in the abstract; full-text behind paywall (J Bacteriol). The Whittam STEC Center website also does not publish per-set counts. Direct contact (stec@cvm.msu.edu) is the documented access path.
- **EAEC, UPEC, ExPEC counts in Horesh 2021** — paper provides EPEC ≈ 3%, ETEC ≈ 2% explicitly; ST11 ≈ 30% (likely UPEC-heavy via E. coli ST11); but explicit EAEC / UPEC / ExPEC pathotype percentages are not in the available excerpt.
- **Outbreak-isolate BioProject inventory** — searched for "curated E. coli outbreak strain BioProject" and got individual BioProject examples (PRJEB39260 bacteremia, PRJNA1120594 One Health Alberta, SRP416785 Bolivia ETEC) but no comprehensive curated inventory of pathotype-labeled outbreak BioProjects. A systematic NCBI BioProject query with `Escherichia coli AND outbreak AND pathotype` would be required to enumerate.
- **EcoCyc DEC reference panel framing was wrong in originating topic** — EcoCyc is a curated database for E. coli K-12 metabolic biology, NOT a pathotype reference panel. The DEC reference panel lives at the Whittam STEC Center / MSU. This is a substrate-naming correction (the project_state ledger references "EcoCyc DEC reference panel" — this should be re-checked and corrected to "Whittam STEC Center DEC collection" if the user intends the diarrheagenic E. coli archive).

## Cross-source summary observations (not citable rows; observations for the user)

- **Pattern: ALL major candidate substrate sources (EnteroBase, NCBI Pathogen Detection, GenomeTrakr, Horesh 2021) use SOME form of gene-rule-derived label assignment** — either as the primary method (EnteroBase BlastFrost) or as a refinement step (Horesh 2021's ariba + VirulenceFinder + isolation source). H1 floor (≥70% independent labels) is at risk on all of them.
- **Independent label sources identified:** (a) clonal-lineage from MLST (used as primary label by DECA + ECOR + von Mentzer ETEC reference); (b) prototype-strain literature labels (EAEC 042, ETEC H10407, EPEC E2348-69, STEC EDL933, EIEC E11 — N=5 each pathotype); (c) `host_disease` and `isolation_source` BioSample fields when populated (free-text in GenomeTrakr; structured-ish in NCBI Pathogen Detection); (d) serotype as a partial proxy (e.g., O157:H7 → EHEC).
- **Cohort feasibility at H2 floors:** Horesh 2021 alone provides ≥200 ETEC + ≥300 EPEC strains; pooled with DECA + von Mentzer ETEC panel + outbreak isolates, N≥50 per pathotype is achievable for EHEC/STEC, EPEC, ETEC, EAEC. UPEC/ExPEC: Horesh 2021's ST11 + ST131 + ST73 + ST95 dominance suggests UPEC/ExPEC counts are HIGH (likely >1000). Commensal-low-marker: harder to determine; requires curating "non-disease isolates" subset.
- **Recommended substrate strategy:** (a) DECA + von Mentzer ETEC + prototype strains as "high-confidence independent-label" core (~100-200 strains; serves H1 PASS bedrock); (b) Horesh 2021 publication-extracted subset as the larger pool (~5,000 strains with provenance metadata); (c) NCBI Pathogen Detection `host_disease`-populated subset as third tier for UPEC/ExPEC + commensal expansion. EnteroBase as last resort — its BlastFrost labels are STRUCTURALLY circular with the v0 deterministic resolver.
