## Honest gaps

- **Direct ΔΔG→MIC (antimicrobial) regression was NOT retrieved** — the antibiotic/antiviral-resistance + MIC search angle was policy-blocked (2 refused queries). The closest public calibration is the ONCOLOGY analog (FEP+ Abl kinase, 88% on competitive kinase-inhibitor binding). Transfer to antimicrobials is an *inference*, not a retrieved result.
- **Binding-only ΔΔG cannot see the dominant non-binding resistance mechanisms**: efflux over-expression, drug-inactivating enzymes (β-lactamases), target amplification/copy-number, promoter/regulatory changes, and catalytic-efficiency (kcat/Km) shifts. A ΔΔG world model is scoped to **target-site competitive-binding** resistance only (e.g. PBP/β-lactam binding, kinase/protease inhibitors), NOT efflux/regulatory cells. (This is the project's own mechanism taxonomy; stated here as scope, not retrieved.)
- **Cheap ddG is a hotspot classifier, not a quantitative fold-change predictor**: RMSE 1.0–1.9 kcal/mol exponentiates to 5–24× fold-change error. FEP-grade (~1.0 kcal/mol MUE) is needed for a real number and even that is ±5×. No public method delivers <2× fold-change accuracy for arbitrary resistance mutations.
- **Structure dependence**: for a genome you'd score off a predicted (AlphaFold/homology) structure; below ~45% template identity cheap ddG collapses toward a null model. Rosetta cartesian_ddg is the more robust cheap option (≥40% identity).
